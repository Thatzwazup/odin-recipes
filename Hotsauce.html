Hot Sauce

Hot sauce is a condiment typically made from chili peppers, vinegar, and various seasonings. The level of spiciness can range from mild to extremely hot

Ingregients

  • 1 cup chili peppers
  • 1 cup vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Optional seasonings (garlic, onion, herbs, etc.)

Instructions

  1. If using fresh chili peppers, wash and dry them. If using dried chili peppers, soak them in hot water for about 20 minutes to soften.
  2. Remove the stems and seeds from the chili peppers, and roughly chop them.
  3. In a small saucepan, combine the chopped chili peppers, vinegar, salt, and sugar. Add any optional seasonings you like.
  4. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 10-15 minutes, or until the peppers are very soft.
  5. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let the mixture cool for a few minutes.
  6. Using an immersion blender or regular blender, blend the mixture until it is smooth.
  7. If the sauce is too thick, add a bit of water or more vinegar to thin it out.
  8. Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning as necessary.
  9. Transfer the hot sauce to a sterilized jar or bottle, and store it in the refrigerator.
